The Kandy City

Located at the center of the Island,72 Miles from Colombo, at an altitude of 488.6m (1629 Feet) above sea level, the city of Kandy is well known both locally and internationally form its scenic beauty.It is the living cultural centre of the country. It was the last stronghold of the Sinhalese Kings until it was finally ceded to the British by agreement in 1815 A.D.

Surrounded by the Hantanne Mountain range, the city has been established in the Valley of the Mahaveli River and covers a land area of 1917 square Kilometers. . The Mahaveli River, which is the longest running river in Sri Lanka, flows on the north, west and east edges of the city. Referred to as “Maha Nuwara,” which means “Great City ”, it is the capital of the Central Province and Kandy District of Sri Lanka. Kandy has become both a residential and commercial hub over the last three to four decades and experienced rapid urban development. The home of the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic of Lord Buddha, the Royal Botanical Gardens(147 acres) at the entrance to the city is one of the best gardens in Asia, with over 5000 species of trees and plants, and a number of other historically and culturally significant sites, it was designated a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1986.
